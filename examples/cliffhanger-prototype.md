# Social Engine — Cliffhanger / Curiosity-Gap Prototype (2026-06-15)

Conversion-first format: open a **genuine** curiosity loop, give the setup, withhold the payoff → resolution lives on the Aktuali site (drives the click + sign-in). Prototyped on the real "free apartment" article. The withheld payoff = **קָפוּא / frozen market** (never shown on-screen).

> **QA flag (native check needed):** this run dotted "developers" as **יַזָּמִים** (ya-za-mím); the source Aktuali card used **יִזְמִים** (yiz-mím). Both forms circulate — flagging the drift so we standardize one. (Same class of issue as the עשה שכונה fix.)

## 1. Curiosity hooks (winner = B)
- A: אֶחָד מִכָּל חֲמִשָּׁה קוֹנִים מְקַבֵּל דִּירָה — בְּחִינָּם. / *One in five buyers gets an apartment — for free.* → a number, not a mystery.
- **B (winner): יַזָּמִים בְּיִשְׂרָאֵל נוֹתְנִים דִּירוֹת בְּמַתָּנָה. לָמָּה שֶׁמִּישֶׁהוּ יַעֲשֶׂה דָּבָר כָּזֶה? / *Developers in Israel are giving away apartments. Why would anyone do that?*** → states the surprise + plants the exact question only the article answers; hero word never leaks.
- C: בִּשְׁבִיל לִמְכֹּר דִּירָה אַחַת, יַזָּמִים נוֹתְנִים דִּירָה שְׁנִיָּה בְּחִינָּם. / *To sell one apartment, developers give a second one away free.* → half-reveals the mechanism.

## 5. Full-resolution vs cliffhanger — usage rule
| | Full-resolution | Cliffhanger |
|---|---|---|
| Viewer gets | Whole story incl. the "why" | Setup + question only; why withheld |
| Creates | Saves, shares, watch-time, replayable teaching | Clicks, profile taps, **sign-ins** |
| Risk | Low conversion | Bounce/fatigue if overused |
| Best for | Vocab-led, explainer, emotionally-complete human-interest | Stories with ONE genuine surprising "why" (this one, scandals, surprising science, "what happened next" sport) |

**Rule of thumb: ~25–35% cliffhangers, rest full-resolution.** Never cliffhanger a story whose "why" is obvious/boring — the loop must be real.

## 6. Curiosity-hook generator (5 reusable patterns)
| # | Pattern | Template | Example (desk) | Clickbait risk / honesty rule |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Paradox** | "[Surprising action] — but why would [actor] do that?" | "A country paid people to *leave* its biggest city — why?" (world) | Low. Paradox must be real; don't manufacture by omitting context. |
| 2 | **The Number That Shouldn't Be** | "[Striking stat]. Doesn't make sense — until you hear the reason." | "1 stock made 40% of all market gains. One reason explains it." (economy) | Medium. Stat accurate; reason must actually resolve it. |
| 3 | **The Reversal** | "Everyone expected X. The opposite happened — the cause surprised even experts." | "Engineers built the fastest chip ever, then quietly shut it off. Why?" (tech) | Med-high. Only if someone was *genuinely* surprised; else drop the clause. |
| 4 | **The Withheld Mechanism** | "[Outcome] is happening. One thing makes it possible." | "An underdog won 12 straight. One halftime change started it." (sport) | Medium. The "one thing" must be the real lede, not a listicle tease. |
| 5 | **The Hidden Stakes** | "[Local event]. It affects [bigger thing] more than you'd think." | "A new rule about cucumbers could change what Europe eats this winter." (economy) | Highest. Cut hype adjectives; the bigger stake must be real & proportionate. |

**Engine guardrail:** the withheld payoff must be a single, real, article-grounded fact (here: קָפוּא). If the engine can't name the specific withheld fact from the article body, the hook is clickbait → reject it and fall back to full-resolution mode.
